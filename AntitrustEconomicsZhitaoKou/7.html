Ministry of Commerce, or MOFCOM, is another Chinese government agency that holds
the antitrust power against merges and acquisition. After one month of effective of Chinese
antitrust law, government started its investigation for its first ever antitrust action. It ruled out
that the acquisition of Huiyuan by Coca-Cola, the popular Chinese juice brand and the American
soda giant, to be illegal. The government said that it gave Coca-Cola too much market power by
combining Chinese juice brand Huiyuan and its own juice brand Minute Maid. Given the fact the
Coca-Cola had a significant market share in a related soft drink market, the MOFCOM believed
that Coca-Cola would have the enough market power thus sell drinks in bundle to eliminate
competitors and hurt consumers .
8
The MOFCOM decision was so controversial that contained a lot of debates. First, the
government concluded that Coca-Cola will sell bundles without any convincible evidence.
Second, the relative market for drinks can also include teas, coffees, milk drinks, sport drinks
and so on. Only calculating the markets for soda and juice will put Coca-Cola a bad position in
determining market power. Third, the government may make its decision based on the policy of
keeping country stable rather than make justice law enforcements. Based on the online poll of
more than 76,000 internet users, more than 80% of them were opposing the deal . The

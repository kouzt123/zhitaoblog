4.5 Counterarguments for NDRC decision
Despite NDRC had tried it best to provide best explanations of its decisions, there are still
some unclear points that may exclude Qualcomm from being convicted.
First, Qualcomm has been setting its patent fees to be 5% for a long history of time.
Regardless what NDRC argued that Qualcomm’s new patents may not be as valuable as expired
patents, it is possible for the new patents to worth more than the old patents. Seeing increasing
number of people to use smartphones, Qualcomm technologies are playing a more important role
in the current world. In this case, Qualcomm set the price to be 5% only to decrease negotiation
cost and promote efficiency with no overcharge at all.
Second, NDRC argued that OEMs were unwilling to license patents back to Qualcomm.
However, with such exchanges of patents, signing a Qualcomm contract was an advantage for
OEMs. It will clear the legal concerns and let OEMs more focused in building great products.
Xiaomi, which is now a Chinese smartphone giant, once experienced massive lawsuit from
Ericsson when entered Indian market. The selling was temporarily banned until Qualcomm
showed up. It turned out all patents that Ericsson sued Xiaomi for were licensed by Qualcomm
.

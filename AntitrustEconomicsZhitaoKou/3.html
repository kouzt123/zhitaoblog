2.1 Qualcomm Inc. Business
The San Diego semiconductor giant Qualcomm, founded in 1985,
is considered one of
the largest semiconductor manufacturers in world. It generates its revenue from selling: 1.
mobile modems, which are used to connect to mobile internet operators; 2. “system on chip”
(SOCs), which is a combination of most popular cell phone parts including modem, computing,
Bluetooth, WiFi, Image Processing Unit and so on; 3. licensing its patents to mobile phone
manufacturers who are normally referred as “Original Equipment Manufacturers” (OEMs). A
great amount of Qualcomm’s patent holdings are considered “standards-essential patents” (SEPs)
which are necessary for mobile devices to connect to the network operators especially
CDMA-based networks, examples include Verizon (US), Sprint (US), China Telecom and so on

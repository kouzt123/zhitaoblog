Even if VR classrooms cannot be used for cross-campus activities in the short time, it is a
smart decision to have it in stock for every college in case of extreme situations. For example, on
December 4, 2017, a huge fire affected the Ventura county, known as the “Thomas Fire”. During
the wildfire, the route from Santa Barbara to Ventura and Los Angeles was cut off. A great
amount of UCSB professors who live outside Santa Barbara county found it impossible to make
it to the school and thus canceled class. In such cases, if they had VR technology on hand, it
would be possible for them to continue their regular class as if nothing happened. They can
quickly create a VR room to exactly match the physical classroom and start the class as usual,
instead of canceling classes and wasting a lot of time. Students are also benefited from using VR
devices at home instead of going to school, given that the outdoor air quality during the Thomas
Fire is near unbreathable.

keeping their price down to average marginal cost.
2.4 The Chinese Antitrust Law
The law, released on 2007 and effective 2008, is one of the youngest antitrust law in the
world. It defines the law closer to the European standards and prohibits the “abuse in
dominance” to ensure the market efficiency. The Chinese government defines “abuse” to be
selling at a price too high, or selling way too cheap, as well as selling with unreasonable trading
conditions. The law also has policies regarding merger control and monopoly agreements.

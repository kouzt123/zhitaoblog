that half of the big companies in China are state-owned companies, the targets of the antitrust
law are more likely to be few private Chinese companies and a lot of foreign companies. The
Chinese way of law enforcement makes it easy to contract a bias. The authorities will choose
cases to investigate and make their decisions first. In the process of selecting targets, it is
possible for them to be more biased toward foreign companies due to nationality, though they do
work on both domestic and international cases. Examples include Chinese alcohol makers
Wuliangye and Maotai, and even state-owned telecom companies China Telecom and China
Unicom.
However, targeting certain Chinese firms does not clear the doubts for foreign
companies. Foreign targets include auto groups in German, software giant Microsoft, and other
multinational corporations. Despite these companies have existing lawsuits that may guide the
Chinese authorities to start investigation, a lot of high profile Chinese cases just walked away
with a easy yes. In the year 2015 alone, there are at least five major mergers include 1.

version of Yelp and Groupon: Dianpng and Meituan; 4. Hotel & flights booking websites: Ctrip

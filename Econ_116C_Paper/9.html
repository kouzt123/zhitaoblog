3.0 Methods
This paper is designed to be a summary of the Qualcomm case in China. Most data will
be drawn from NDRC decisions as well as Qualcomm reports. The paper also includes additional
data from reliable sources to further illustrate NDRC decisions. Some data come from sources
related to other Qualcomm lawsuits. Examples include public information released by Federal
Trade Commission (FTC) in U.S., and only the sources helpful to understand the Chinese case
will be included. To discuss the legitimate reason behind the NDRC decision, this paper also
include some other Chinese government decisions as well.
3.0 Findings
3.1 Qualcomm modem market share
Qualcomm, as the major inventor of CDMA network from last century and continues
developments over the years, owns significant amount of patents in CDMA, WCDMA, and LTE
technologies. According to the data from Strategy Analytics conducted in 2013, which is also
quoted in NDRC decisions, Qualcomm has a revenue-based market share of 93.1% in CDMA
markets, 53.9% in WCDMA markets, and 96% in LTE markets. Qualcomm has a significant
market share, defined as a share of greater than 50%, in all these three markets. In addition,

3.3 Qualcomm’s “umbrella”
Qualcomm has another theory for its patent fees. In practice, Qualcomm may ask the
contractor to license its related patent holdings back to Qualcomm for free
, as well as give up

not take into consideration the value of the clients’ patents, neither pay nor make discount for the
clients. Under the pressure of Qualcomm’s “no patent no chip” policy, most OEMs agreed to
these terms. By distributing these terms, Qualcomm lowered the barriers for the OEM market.
Such actions are often considered an “umbrella” of protection to Qualcomm clients, especially
small clients. Since small firms will not be sued by large companies for intellectual properties as

entry. For example, Cui Jian, a Chinese singer, released his own named smartphone in April

2013. Despite these phones did not sell well, it demonstrates the idea of low entry barriers for
smartphone market. Exceptions of these examples include Luo Yonghao, a popular English
teacher, created his smartphone company “smartisan” and received a limited success because of
his obsessed attitudes toward industrial design.
